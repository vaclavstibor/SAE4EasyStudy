## About

The aim of this study is to evaluate two different ways of interacting with a **movie recommender system**: (1) a standard recommender that just shows a list of suggestions, and (2) a version that lets **you** steer the recommendations using controllable features (either **sliders** for different movie aspects or a short **text prompt**).

You will see both versions in random order and tell us, using a short questionnaire after each one, how accurate, diverse, satisfying and controllable the recommendations felt. We are especially interested in whether the controllable version actually makes you feel *more in control* of what the system shows you.

Please read this description carefully before starting the study.

## Study procedure

The study has the following main parts:

1. **Consent form.** After opening the link, you'll see a short form explaining what data we collect and how we use it. Please read it and agree to participate.
2. **Preference elicitation (select your favorites).** You will pick **at least 5–10 movies** you know and like from a grid. You can also search by title. These selections personalize the recommendations you'll see next.
3. **Phase A — first recommender.** You will explore a list of recommended movies, mark the ones you'd watch later, and — depending on the randomly assigned version — either just browse (baseline) or refine the list using **sliders** (steered). After the phase, you fill in a short **Likert questionnaire** about accuracy, diversity, control, ease of use, and overall satisfaction.
4. **Phase B — second recommender.** The other version of the recommender. Same procedure: explore, select, (optionally) steer, and answer the same questionnaire so we can compare the two versions within the same participant.
5. **Final questionnaire.** A few broader questions about your overall experience and free-text feedback.
6. **Finish.** Click "**Finish user study**" on the final page. You will be **automatically redirected back to Prolific** with your completion code. Please wait for the redirect — do not close the tab manually, otherwise your submission may not be recorded.

## Attention checks

The study contains a small number of **attention-check items** embedded in the questionnaires. They exist to make sure your answers are considered carefully. Submissions that fail multiple attention checks may be **returned or rejected**, so please read each question before answering.

## Remember

- Follow the instructions shown at the top of each page.
- **Do not use the browser's Back button** once you move forward — going back is disabled in this study.
- Trust your first impressions; don't overthink the ratings.
- Keep the browser tab in the foreground until the final redirect.
- The recommendations come from a **fixed catalog** of movies; the newest titles in our dataset are from 2018.
